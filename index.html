Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is an Inca citadel located high in the Andes Mountains in Peru, above the Urubamba River Valley. Built in the 15th century and later abandoned, it is famous for its sophisticated dry-stone walls, which combine enormous blocks without mortar; its fascinating buildings linked to astronomical alignments; and its panoramic views.
